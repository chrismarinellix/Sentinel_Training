üìö Introduction to AEMO GPS Process

The Australian Energy Market Operator (AEMO) requires all new generating systems connecting to the National Electricity Market (NEM) to demonstrate compliance with Generator Performance Standards (GPS) as defined in the National Electricity Rules (NER). This process ensures that new generation maintains power system security and reliability.

üí° What is GPS?

Generator Performance Standards are technical requirements that generating systems must meet to connect to and operate within the NEM. These standards cover aspects including voltage control, frequency control, active and reactive power capability, response to system disturbances, and power quality (including harmonics).

üîÑ NEM Clause 5.2.x Overview

NEM Clause 5.2 outlines the connection application process. The key stages relevant to GPS documentation include:

  • Clause 5.2.2: Preliminary enquiry - Initial discussions with AEMO regarding connection
  • Clause 5.2.3: Connection application - Formal application to connect generating system
  • Clause 5.2.5: Performance standards - Negotiation and agreement of performance standards
  • Clause 5.2.6: Conditions for connection - Technical conditions and commissioning requirements
  • Clause 5.2.7: Commissioning and testing - Validation of GPS compliance through testing

üîß Clause 5.2.5 - Performance Standards Detail

Clause 5.2.5 is the most critical section for GPS compliance. Under this clause, the generator must:

  • Propose performance standards that at minimum meet the automatic access standards
  • Negotiate any variations from automatic access standards with AEMO
  • Provide technical studies demonstrating compliance with proposed standards
  • Address AEMO's technical requirements and concerns through iterative submissions

üìÑ GPS Package Contents

A complete GPS submission package typically includes the following documents and technical studies:

Core Documentation

  • GPS Template: Completed AEMO GPS template with all performance standards declared
  • Project Description: Detailed description of generating system, technology, and configuration
  • Single Line Diagram: Electrical schematic showing connection arrangement
  • Equipment Specifications: Technical datasheets for all major equipment (inverters, transformers, etc.)

Technical Studies

  • Steady-State Analysis: Load flow studies showing voltage and power flow under various scenarios
  • Short Circuit Analysis: Fault level contribution calculations
  • Harmonics Study: Assessment of harmonic emissions and compliance with limits
  • Voltage Flicker Study: Analysis of voltage fluctuations during operation
  • Reactive Power Capability: Demonstration of Q capability across operating range
  • Disturbance Ride-Through: Evidence of compliance with fault ride-through requirements

Control System Documentation

  • Control Philosophy: Description of control systems and operating modes
  • Protection Settings: Relay settings and coordination studies
  • SCADA/Communications: Communications architecture and data points

‚úÖ Best Practice Tip

Start the GPS package preparation early in the project lifecycle. Many studies require detailed equipment information that may not be available until procurement is advanced. Coordinate closely with equipment suppliers to obtain necessary technical data and simulation models.

‚ö° Harmonics Analysis - Deep Dive

Harmonics analysis is a critical component of GPS submissions, particularly for inverter-based generation (solar, wind, BESS). This study assesses whether the project will comply with harmonic voltage and current limits at the connection point.

Regulatory Framework

  • NER S5.2.3: Automatic access standard for power quality (voltage harmonics)
  • AS/NZS 61000.3.6: Assessment of emission limits for the connection of distorting installations
  • AEMO Planning Criteria: Network planning standards for power quality

Harmonics Study Methodology

A comprehensive harmonics study for GPS submission typically follows these steps:

  1. Network Model Development: Build detailed model of connection network in software (PSCAD, DIgSILENT, PSSE)
  2. Background Harmonics: Include existing background harmonic levels from TNSP data
  3. Source Modeling: Model inverter harmonic emissions using manufacturer data or IEEE 1547 defaults
  4. Resonance Assessment: Identify potential parallel/series resonance points in network
  5. Worst-Case Scenarios: Simulate various operating conditions (full output, partial output, network configurations)
  6. Harmonic Summation: Calculate individual and total harmonic distortion at connection point
  7. Compliance Assessment: Compare results against applicable limits
  8. Mitigation (if required): Design filters or other mitigation measures if limits exceeded

üîß Real Project Example: Solar Farm Harmonics Study

Project: 200MW Solar Farm connecting at 132kV
Challenge: Initial studies showed 7th harmonic voltage exceeded planning level (1.8% vs 1.6% limit)
Root Cause: Network parallel resonance near 7th harmonic due to combination of line capacitance and transformer inductance
Solution: Modified transformer specification to detune resonance point; final study showed compliance with 1.4% THD
Key Learning: Early engagement with TNSP on network parameters critical for accurate modeling

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Harmonics Study Pitfalls

  • Inadequate network model: Using simplified network can miss resonance conditions
  • Generic inverter data: Using default values rather than project-specific manufacturer data
  • Ignoring background harmonics: Failure to include existing network harmonic levels
  • Limited operating scenarios: Only modeling full output rather than range of conditions
  • Phase angle neglect: Not considering phase relationships in harmonic summation

üîÑ AEMO Review and RFI Process

After GPS package submission, AEMO conducts a detailed technical review. This typically results in one or more Requests for Information (RFI) requiring additional clarification or studies.

Typical AEMO Review Timeline

  • Initial Review: 20-30 business days from submission
  • RFI Response: 15-20 business days for proponent response
  • Follow-up Review: 10-15 business days for AEMO assessment
  • Iterative Process: 2-4 RFI rounds typical for complex projects
  • Total Duration: 3-6 months from initial submission to final acceptance

üí° Managing the RFI Process

Successful navigation of the RFI process requires clear communication and thorough responses:

  • Respond to each RFI point systematically with clear references to supporting documentation
  • Provide additional analysis or clarification where requested rather than simply restating previous submissions
  • Engage in pre-submission discussions with AEMO to understand their concerns
  • Keep client informed of RFI status and any implications for project schedule
  • Document all assumptions and methodologies clearly to minimize back-and-forth

‚úÖ Commissioning and Testing

Following GPS acceptance, the project proceeds to commissioning phase where GPS compliance must be validated through testing:

Key GPS Commissioning Tests

  • Active Power Capability: Demonstrate full MW output capability
  • Reactive Power Capability: Verify Q capability across specified operating range
  • Voltage Control: Test voltage regulation and response to setpoint changes
  • Frequency Control: Validate frequency response and droop settings
  • Disturbance Ride-Through: Demonstrate fault ride-through capability (may use simulation if testing not feasible)
  • Power Quality: Measure harmonic emissions during operation and verify compliance
  • Protection Systems: Test protection schemes and coordination

‚úÖ Commissioning Best Practice

Develop a detailed commissioning test plan early in the project and submit to AEMO for review. This ensures alignment on test methodologies and acceptance criteria before commissioning begins. Include contingency plans for tests that may not achieve target performance on first attempt.

üîã 200MW/400MWh Battery Energy Storage System

Location: Victoria, 220kV connection
Issue: High frequency harmonics (25th-49th) approaching limits due to PWM switching frequency

Outcome: Demonstrated compliance after including battery filter impedance in model; AEMO accepted EMT study approach
Timeline Impact: Additional EMT study added 6 weeks to GPS process
Key Lesson: For BESS projects, anticipate need for high-frequency harmonic analysis beyond standard frequency-domain studies

Resolution: Provided time-domain simulation results showing voltage recovery within specified timeframes

‚ö†Ô∏è Project Schedule Implications

GPS process duration can significantly impact project schedules. Build adequate contingency:

  • Simple projects: 3-4 months from submission to acceptance
  • Standard projects: 4-6 months including typical RFI rounds
  • Complex projects: 6-9 months for weak networks or novel technologies
  • Critical path: GPS acceptance typically required before final commissioning stages

üéØ Key Takeaways

‚úÖ Success Factors for GPS Submissions

  1. Early Engagement: Discuss project with AEMO early to understand specific concerns
  2. Complete Documentation: Ensure all required studies and documents included in initial submission
  3. Quality Over Speed: Thorough initial submission reduces RFI rounds and overall timeline
  4. Technical Rigor: Use appropriate modeling tools and methodologies for each study
  5. Clear Communication: Present results clearly with well-structured reports and supporting data
  6. Equipment Coordination: Work closely with suppliers to obtain accurate technical data
  7. Lessons Learned: Apply learnings from previous projects and industry experience
  8. Schedule Management: Build realistic timeline with contingency for review process
